Overview

What Is the California Welfare Exemption?

The California Welfare Exemption (Revenue and Taxation Code Section 214) provides a full property tax exemption for real property owned and operated by qualifying nonprofit organizations for charitable purposes — including affordable housing development and operation.

For affordable housing developers, this exemption can eliminate 100% of property taxes on qualifying units, creating hundreds of thousands of dollars in annual savings that directly improve project economics and financing terms.

Key Eligibility Requirements

  • Nonprofit ownership: Property must be owned by a 501(c)(3) organization (or the nonprofit must be the managing general partner in a limited partnership)
  • Charitable purpose: Property must be used exclusively for affordable housing — serving tenants at or below 80% of Area Median Income (AMI)
  • Rent restrictions: Rents must not exceed limits published by the California Department of Housing and Community Development (HCD)
  • Organizational test: The nonprofit's articles of incorporation and operations must demonstrate a primary charitable purpose
  • Annual filing: BOE-267 (Claim for Organizational Clearance Certificate) must be filed with the county assessor, with annual recertification

How It Affects Your Financing

The Welfare Exemption has a direct, positive impact on every aspect of your project's financing:

  • Higher NOI: Eliminating property taxes increases Net Operating Income, improving cash flow available for debt service
  • Stronger DSCR: Higher NOI means a higher Debt Service Coverage Ratio, which helps you qualify for larger loan amounts
  • Better loan terms: Lenders view welfare-exempt properties favorably because the tax savings are reliable and recurring
  • Larger proceeds: With improved cash flow metrics, you may qualify for 5–15% more in loan proceeds compared to a taxed scenario
