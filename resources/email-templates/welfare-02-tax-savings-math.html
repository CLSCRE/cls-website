How the Welfare Exemption Saves $250K+/Year on a $20M Project

In your Welfare Exemption Guide, we touched on the financial impact of a successful exemption. Today, let us walk through the math on a real-world scenario.

The Scenario

A nonprofit developer builds a 120-unit affordable housing community in Southern California. Total project cost: $20 million. Assessed value for property tax purposes: approximately $20 million.

Metric Without Exemption With Exemption
Assessed Value $20,000,000 $0
Effective Tax Rate ~1.25% 0%
Annual Property Tax $250,000 $0

$250,000 saved annually flows directly to NOI — improving your DSCR, increasing available debt proceeds, and strengthening your application with every lender in the market.

Why This Matters for Financing

That $250K improvement in NOI does not just reduce expenses. At a 1.20x DSCR requirement and a 6% interest rate, it can support approximately $3.5 million in additional loan proceeds. That is the difference between a deal that pencils and one that does not.

We work with life insurance companies, CDFI lenders, and agency platforms that specifically underwrite affordable housing with the welfare exemption factored into their analysis — and we know which ones will give you the most credit for it.
