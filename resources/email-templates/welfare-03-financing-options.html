How We Finance Affordable Housing in California

California's affordable housing capital stack is more complex than conventional commercial real estate — but that complexity creates opportunity. When you layer the right programs together, you can achieve leverage and terms that conventional borrowers cannot access.

Here are the primary financing tools we work with:

Low-Income Housing Tax Credits (LIHTC)

The cornerstone of affordable housing finance. 4% credits pair with tax-exempt bonds for larger projects; 9% credits are competitive but provide deeper subsidy. We work with syndicators and investors to ensure your tax credit equity is maximized.

Tax-Exempt Bonds

Issued through state or local housing finance agencies, these bonds offer below-market interest rates and automatically qualify the project for 4% LIHTC. We have relationships with multiple bond issuers and underwriters across the state.

CDFI and Mission-Driven Lenders

Community Development Financial Institutions offer flexible predevelopment, acquisition, and construction loans specifically for affordable housing. Many offer below-market rates and more flexible underwriting for mission-aligned projects.

Permanent Loans (FHA/Agency)

FHA 221(d)(4) for construction/permanent, Freddie Mac TEL for tax-exempt loan takeouts, and Fannie Mae affordable programs all provide long-term, low-rate financing. We source from agency lenders who specialize in affordable multifamily.

The welfare exemption amplifies every one of these programs by improving your NOI and debt coverage. It is a critical piece of the puzzle — and one that we make sure every lender sees in the package.
