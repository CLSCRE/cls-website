What Lenders Look For in 2026

Whether you are refinancing a stabilized asset or acquiring a new property, understanding what lenders prioritize will help you prepare a stronger application — and ultimately get better terms.

Here are the three metrics that matter most in today's lending environment:

Debt Service Coverage Ratio (DSCR)

The single most important underwriting metric. DSCR measures whether the property's net operating income covers the annual debt payments.

Minimum threshold: 1.20x – 1.25x for most lenders
Best rates at: 1.35x+ DSCR
Pro tip: Some lenders use stressed rates (higher than actual) — we know which ones use actual rates

Loan-to-Value (LTV)

How much of the property's value the lender will finance. Higher LTV means less equity required — but it comes with trade-offs on rate and terms.

Conservative (best rate): 55%–65% LTV
Standard: 65%–75% LTV
High leverage: 75%–80% LTV (SBA, bridge, select agency programs)

Sponsor Experience and Liquidity

Lenders underwrite the borrower as much as the property. Track record with similar assets, net worth, and post-close liquidity all factor into pricing and approval.

Net worth: Typically 1x the loan amount requested
Liquidity: 10%–15% of loan amount post-close
Experience: Similar property type and market preferred
