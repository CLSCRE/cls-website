Specialty Financing FAQ

Specialty properties include asset types outside the traditional multifamily, industrial, retail, office, and hospitality categories. Examples include self-storage, data centers, marinas, religious facilities, entertainment venues, car washes, funeral homes, and other niche property types with specialized operations.
Specialty properties can be more challenging to finance because fewer lenders have experience underwriting them. However, with the right broker relationships and deal structuring, competitive financing is available. CLS CRE's network of 1,000+ lenders includes specialists in virtually every niche property type.
Rates for specialty properties vary widely depending on the asset class, ranging from 5.54% for well-established assets like self-storage to 13%+ for more niche or transitional specialty properties. Strong operating history and creditworthy tenants help achieve better terms.
Non-recourse financing is available for select specialty property types, particularly self-storage, data centers, and properties with credit-tenant net leases. More niche assets may require recourse or partial recourse guarantees.
In addition to standard commercial loan documentation, specialty property lenders typically require detailed operating statements showing business performance, management agreements, franchise or license documentation, and industry-specific underwriting metrics relevant to the asset class.
