Commercial Real Estate Market Report: Nashville 2026

By Trevor Damyan November 14, 2025

Nashville has emerged as one of the nation's most dynamic commercial real estate markets, driven by healthcare industry dominance (HCA Healthcare, Community Health Systems, Vanderbilt Health), a thriving entertainment and tourism economy, and accelerating technology sector growth. The metro's no state income tax, pro-business environment, and exceptional quality of life continue to attract corporate relocations and young professionals at a pace that ranks among the fastest in the Southeast.

Nashville Market Overview: Key Metrics

The Nashville commercial real estate market in 2026 reflects a market shaped by healthcare, entertainment, technology, automotive, tourism. Here are the key metrics investors and borrowers should know:

  • Multifamily Vacancy: 5.5% — near the national average with healthy absorption
  • Industrial Vacancy: 4.8% — among the tightest markets nationally
  • Office Vacancy: 15.8%
  • Retail Vacancy: 3.8%
  • Rent Growth: 3.5% year-over-year
  • Job Growth: 2.8% — outpacing the national average
  • Population Growth: 1.5% annually
  • Median Asking Rent: $1,650

Multifamily Outlook in Nashville

Nashville multifamily fundamentals are among the strongest in the Sun Belt, with vacancy at 5.5% and rent growth at 3.5% year-over-year. The Gulch, Germantown, and East Nashville command premium rents driven by walkability and proximity to entertainment and dining. The construction pipeline has moderated from 2023 peaks, and absorption has been strong as the metro continues to add approximately 100 residents per day.

Industrial & Logistics Market

Nashville's industrial market benefits from the metro's central Southeast location and growing logistics infrastructure, with vacancy at just 4.8%. Healthcare supply chain and distribution operations are a unique demand driver, complementing traditional e-commerce and manufacturing tenants. The I-24 corridor and Wilson County represent the primary industrial investment nodes, with new interstate improvements expanding available development sites.

Office & Retail Dynamics

Nashville's office market has outperformed most major metros at 15.8% vacancy, driven by healthcare corporate headquarters and a growing technology and professional services sector that values the market's talent base and quality of life. Retail is Nashville's standout sector at just 3.8% vacancy, supported by extraordinary tourism traffic (over 14 million visitors annually) and the metro's booming restaurant and entertainment scene.

Financing Landscape in Nashville

Nashville's lending market has matured significantly as the metro gains institutional recognition. Agency multifamily pricing is competitive, and life companies are increasingly allocating capital to Nashville industrial and office assets. Tennessee-based banks and credit unions provide additional depth, particularly for transactions under $15 million where local relationships and market knowledge create competitive advantages.

For borrowers in the Nashville-Davidson-Murfreesboro-Franklin area, current commercial mortgage rates range from 5.25% for agency multifamily to higher rates for transitional and value-add projects. Key factors that influence your rate include property type, leverage, sponsor experience, and asset location within the metro.

Top Submarkets to Watch

The Nashville metro features several distinct submarkets that present unique investment opportunities:

  • The Gulch
  • East Nashville
  • Germantown
  • Franklin
  • Murfreesboro
  • Brentwood

Each of these submarkets has distinct characteristics in terms of tenant demand, development activity, and pricing. The top investment corridors in Nashville include The Gulch mixed-use, East Nashville creative, Franklin corporate, Murfreesboro growth.

Investment Outlook: Nashville 2026

Nashville is positioned for continued outperformance in 2026, driven by healthcare sector stability, entertainment tourism growth, and accelerating technology investment. The market's relatively moderate supply pipeline compared to Austin and Phoenix suggests tighter conditions ahead. Top opportunities include The Gulch and Germantown multifamily, I-24 corridor industrial, and retail assets in high-traffic entertainment and tourism corridors.
