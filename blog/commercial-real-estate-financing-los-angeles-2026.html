Commercial Real Estate Financing in Los Angeles: 2026 Market Guide

By Trevor Damyan March 15, 2026

Los Angeles remains one of the most dynamic commercial real estate markets in the United States. With over $15 billion in annual CRE transaction volume, a population of nearly 4 million in the city proper (and 13 million across the metro), and an economy that would rank among the world's largest countries, LA demands a financing approach as sophisticated as the market itself. This guide covers everything borrowers and investors need to know about securing commercial real estate financing in Los Angeles in 2026.

Los Angeles Commercial Real Estate Market Overview: 2026

The LA commercial real estate market entering 2026 is defined by resilience and selective opportunity. After navigating elevated interest rates through 2023-2024, transaction volume has rebounded as spreads compress and capital returns to the market.

Cap Rates and Valuations

Cap rates across Los Angeles vary significantly by property type and submarket. Multifamily (Class A): 4.25%-5.00%. Multifamily (Class B/C): 5.00%-6.25%. Industrial: 4.50%-5.75%. Retail (grocery-anchored): 5.75%-7.00%. Office (Class A, West Side): 6.00%-7.50%. Mixed-use: 5.00%-6.50%.

Vacancy and Absorption Trends

Multifamily vacancy sits at roughly 4.5% metro-wide. Industrial vacancy has ticked up slightly to 5.5% from historic lows. Office vacancy at approximately 18-20%. Retail has stabilized with vacancy near 5.5%.

Hot LA Submarkets for Commercial Investment in 2026

Downtown LA has evolved into a true live-work-play urban core with over 90,000 residents. Koreatown ranks among the densest neighborhoods in the western United States. South Bay and Inland Gateway industrial properties continue attracting institutional capital. West LA remains the premier office submarket. San Fernando Valley offers relative value.

Loan Programs Available for Los Angeles Commercial Properties

Agency Multifamily (Fannie/Freddie): 5.25%-6.00%. CMBS Conduit: 6.00%-7.25%. Bridge Loans: 8.00%-11.00%. SBA 504 (blended): 5.00%-6.50%. Construction: 7.00%-10.00%. DSCR Investor: 6.50%-9.00%. Mezzanine: 10%-15%.

Frequently Asked Questions

As of March 2026, commercial real estate loan rates in Los Angeles range from 5.25% to 7.00% for permanent financing, 8.00% to 11.00% for bridge loans, and 5.00% to 6.50% blended for SBA 504 loans. Multifamily properties in prime LA submarkets command the most competitive rates.
LA borrowers have access to agency multifamily loans (Fannie Mae/Freddie Mac), CMBS conduit loans, life company permanent financing, commercial bridge loans, SBA 504 and 7(a) loans, construction loans, mezzanine financing, and DSCR investor loans.
Top LA submarkets for CRE investment in 2026 include Downtown LA (DTLA) for multifamily and mixed-use, the Arts District for creative and industrial conversion, Koreatown for multifamily density, West LA and Century City for office, and the South Bay for industrial and logistics properties.
A local LA commercial mortgage broker understands submarket-level dynamics, maintains relationships with regional and national lenders active in the market, knows which lenders favor specific property types or neighborhoods, and can navigate LA-specific requirements like seismic retrofitting and rent control considerations.
Closing timelines in LA vary by loan type: bridge loans close in 15-30 days, SBA loans in 60-90 days, conventional permanent loans in 45-75 days, and agency multifamily loans in 30-60 days.
