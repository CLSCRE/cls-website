Commercial Real Estate Market Report: Seattle 2026

By Trevor Damyan November 06, 2025

Seattle is one of the nation's premier technology-driven commercial real estate markets, home to Amazon, Microsoft, Boeing, and a deep ecosystem of cloud computing, AI, and software companies. The metro's constrained geography — bounded by water and mountains — creates natural supply limitations that support property values, while the concentration of high-income technology workers drives premium rents across multifamily, retail, and mixed-use assets.

Seattle Market Overview: Key Metrics

The Seattle commercial real estate market in 2026 reflects a market shaped by technology, aerospace, healthcare, e-commerce, cloud computing. Here are the key metrics investors and borrowers should know:

  • Multifamily Vacancy: 5.8% — near the national average with healthy absorption
  • Industrial Vacancy: 4.5% — among the tightest markets nationally
  • Office Vacancy: 19.5%
  • Retail Vacancy: 4.2%
  • Rent Growth: 3.5% year-over-year
  • Job Growth: 2.0% — tracking near the national average
  • Population Growth: 0.8% annually
  • Median Asking Rent: $2,050

Multifamily Outlook in Seattle

Seattle multifamily fundamentals are strong, with vacancy at 5.8% and rent growth at 3.5%, driven by the metro's high-income renter base and constrained housing supply. South Lake Union, Capitol Hill, and First Hill command the highest rents, while Bellevue and the East Side have emerged as premium markets in their own right, fueled by technology company campuses and excellent transit access via the new light rail extension.

Industrial & Logistics Market

Seattle's industrial market is one of the tightest on the West Coast at 4.5% vacancy, constrained by geographic limitations and intense demand from e-commerce (Amazon's home market), port-related logistics, and aerospace manufacturing. The Kent Valley, Fife/Tacoma, and South Seattle submarkets serve as the primary industrial nodes, with rents that have nearly doubled over the past five years.

Office & Retail Dynamics

The Seattle office market is in transition at 19.5% vacancy, as technology companies right-size their footprints post-pandemic. However, Bellevue's East Side office market has outperformed, attracting Amazon, Meta, and Google expansions that have absorbed significant space. Retail fundamentals are excellent at 4.2% vacancy, supported by the metro's affluent consumer base and strong tourism.

Financing Landscape in Seattle

Seattle's lending market benefits from the presence of major Pacific Northwest-based banks and a strong national lender presence. The market's premium pricing and technology-driven economy attract institutional debt capital across all property types. Agency multifamily execution is efficient, while life companies and CMBS conduits target industrial and well-located mixed-use assets.

For borrowers in the Seattle-Tacoma-Bellevue area, current commercial mortgage rates range from 4.75% for agency multifamily to higher rates for transitional and value-add projects. Key factors that influence your rate include property type, leverage, sponsor experience, and asset location within the metro.

Top Submarkets to Watch

The Seattle metro features several distinct submarkets that present unique investment opportunities:

  • South Lake Union
  • Capitol Hill
  • Bellevue
  • Tacoma
  • Redmond
  • Shoreline

Each of these submarkets has distinct characteristics in terms of tenant demand, development activity, and pricing. The top investment corridors in Seattle include South Lake Union tech campus, Bellevue East Side, Kent Valley industrial, Capitol Hill multifamily.

Investment Outlook: Seattle 2026

Seattle remains a top-tier market for long-term CRE investment, supported by the technology sector's structural growth, constrained supply, and a high-income labor force. The strongest opportunities in 2026 include East Side multifamily (tech campus proximity), Kent Valley industrial (irreplaceable logistics infrastructure), and selective office in Bellevue where technology tenant demand continues to absorb space.
