Commercial Real Estate Market Report: Austin 2026

By Trevor Damyan February 14, 2026

Austin's commercial real estate market is experiencing a recalibration after years of explosive growth that attracted massive new development. The metro remains one of the fastest-growing in the nation at 2.1% population growth, with a technology sector anchored by Tesla, Samsung, Apple, and a deep startup ecosystem. While near-term supply headwinds are real, Austin's structural demand drivers — job growth, migration, and economic diversification — remain compelling.

Austin Market Overview: Key Metrics

The Austin commercial real estate market in 2026 reflects a market shaped by technology, semiconductor manufacturing, government, healthcare, education. Here are the key metrics investors and borrowers should know:

  • Multifamily Vacancy: 8.5% — above the national average as new supply is absorbed
  • Industrial Vacancy: 7.2% — normalizing as speculative development is absorbed
  • Office Vacancy: 19.8%
  • Retail Vacancy: 4.2%
  • Rent Growth: 1.8% year-over-year
  • Job Growth: 2.8% — outpacing the national average
  • Population Growth: 2.1% annually
  • Median Asking Rent: $1,575

Multifamily Outlook in Austin

Austin multifamily faces the most significant supply challenge of any major Sun Belt market, with vacancy at 8.5% as an unprecedented wave of new deliveries competes for tenants. Rent growth has slowed to 1.8%, and concessions are common in newer Class A properties. However, construction starts have plummeted, indicating that the supply overhang is peaking and should moderate meaningfully by late 2026. Value-oriented investors view current conditions as a buying opportunity.

Industrial & Logistics Market

Austin's industrial market has absorbed significant new supply, with vacancy at 7.2%. The market's primary demand drivers include semiconductor manufacturing (Samsung's $17 billion fabrication facility), technology hardware, and e-commerce distribution. The I-35 corridor and East Austin represent the primary industrial nodes, with growing demand from advanced manufacturing supporting tenant quality and lease terms.

Office & Retail Dynamics

The Austin office market reflects the national trend amplified by the metro's startup culture, with vacancy at 19.8% as technology companies adjust headcounts and space utilization. The Domain and Downtown corridors remain the most sought-after locations. Retail is the bright spot at 4.2% vacancy, driven by Austin's extraordinary population growth that creates continuous demand for neighborhood services and dining.

Financing Landscape in Austin

Austin's lending market has become more selective as lenders adjust to the supply environment, particularly for multifamily where underwriting assumptions require more conservative rent growth projections. Agency lenders remain active but have tightened market-level standards. Bridge lending is available for well-structured deals with experienced sponsors who can demonstrate clear value creation strategies.

For borrowers in the Austin-Round Rock-Georgetown area, current commercial mortgage rates range from 5.00% for agency multifamily to higher rates for transitional and value-add projects. Key factors that influence your rate include property type, leverage, sponsor experience, and asset location within the metro.

Top Submarkets to Watch

The Austin metro features several distinct submarkets that present unique investment opportunities:

  • Downtown
  • East Austin
  • The Domain
  • Cedar Park
  • Round Rock
  • Georgetown

Each of these submarkets has distinct characteristics in terms of tenant demand, development activity, and pricing. The top investment corridors in Austin include Domain/North Austin tech, East Austin creative, Round Rock suburban, Georgetown growth.

Investment Outlook: Austin 2026

Austin's long-term investment thesis remains intact — the metro's technology economy, population growth, and quality of life continue to attract employers and residents at a pace that will absorb current supply overhangs. Near-term opportunities exist for contrarian investors willing to acquire at cyclically wide cap rates and benefit from the market's eventual tightening. The strongest sectors are industrial (semiconductor ecosystem), grocery-anchored retail, and discounted multifamily with basis protection.
