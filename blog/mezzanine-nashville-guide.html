Mezzanine & Preferred Equity in Nashville: What Borrowers Need to Know

By Trevor Damyan December 25, 2025

Mezzanine and preferred equity in Nashville serve sponsors pursuing multifamily and mixed-use acquisitions in a market experiencing rapid institutional capital inflows. The metro's population growth and job creation support strong subordinate capital returns, with national debt funds increasingly active as Nashville's investment profile has grown to match larger Southern metros.

When to Use Mezzanine & Preferred Equity in Nashville

Nashville's commercial real estate market, driven by healthcare, entertainment, technology, automotive, tourism, creates specific scenarios where mezzanine & preferred equity are the optimal financing choice:

  • High-leverage acquisitions
  • Development projects needing additional capital
  • Value-add strategies with equity gap
  • Recapitalizations and cash-out scenarios
  • Joint venture equity structures
  • Portfolio-level capital solutions

In the Nashville-Davidson-Murfreesboro-Franklin metro, mezzanine & preferred equity are particularly relevant given the market's 3.5% rent growth and 2.8% job growth, which support higher-leverage capital structures for competitive acquisitions.

Current Mezzanine Loan Rates in Nashville

As of 2026, mezzanine & preferred equity in the Nashville market are pricing at the following levels:

  • Rate Range: 10% - 18%
  • Loan Amount: $1M - $50M+
  • Term: 1 - 5 Years
  • Total Leverage: Up to 85-90% LTC
  • Recourse:

Rates in Nashville may vary from national averages based on local market conditions, property type, and sponsor experience. The Nashville market's 5.25%-5.75% multifamily cap rates and 5.50%-6.00% industrial cap rates influence lender pricing as they underwrite to specific debt yield and coverage targets.

Qualification Requirements

Qualifying for mezzanine & preferred equity in Nashville requires demonstrating both borrower strength and property fundamentals. Key requirements include:

  • Borrower Experience: Lenders evaluate your track record with similar assets in Nashville or comparable markets
  • Net Worth & Liquidity: Most lenders require net worth equal to the loan amount and 6-12 months of debt service in liquid reserves
  • Property Performance: Existing senior debt in place, property cash flow or value-add plan supporting the combined capital stack
  • Market Position: Asset location within Nashville's strongest submarkets, including The Gulch mixed-use, East Nashville creative, Franklin corporate, Murfreesboro growth

Capital Sources for Mezzanine Loans in Nashville

The Nashville market offers access to a diverse set of capital sources for mezzanine & preferred equity:

  • Debt Funds
  • Private Equity Firms
  • Family Offices
  • Insurance Companies
  • Specialty Lenders

Each capital source has distinct appetites for property types, leverage levels, and borrower profiles. Working with a commercial mortgage broker who maintains relationships across all these capital sources ensures you're seeing the most competitive terms available in Nashville.

Exit Strategy Considerations

Mezzanine and preferred equity positions in Nashville are structured with clear exit timelines, typically aligning with the business plan execution period. The exit is usually through a refinance that consolidates the capital stack at a lower blended cost once the property's value has increased, or through a property sale that generates sufficient proceeds to repay all capital layers.

Given Nashville's 3.5% rent growth, well-executed value-add strategies can create the equity cushion needed to refinance into permanent financing that fully repays the mezzanine position.

Nashville Market Context

Nashville has emerged as a top-tier commercial real estate market, driven by healthcare industry dominance, entertainment tourism, and strong in-migration. The metro's pro-business environment, no state income tax, and quality of life attract both employers and residents, supporting robust demand across multifamily, industrial, and office sectors.

Understanding the local market dynamics is critical for structuring the right financing. The Nashville metro's key commercial neighborhoods include The Gulch, East Nashville, Germantown, Franklin, Murfreesboro, Brentwood, each with distinct property characteristics and tenant demand profiles.
