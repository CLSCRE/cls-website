Mezzanine & Preferred Equity in Seattle: What Borrowers Need to Know

By Trevor Damyan March 20, 2026

Mezzanine and preferred equity in Seattle support sponsors pursuing value-add multifamily and mixed-use acquisitions in a market defined by tech-driven demand and limited land availability. The metro's low vacancy and strong rent growth create favorable conditions for subordinate capital, with national and regional debt funds active in the Puget Sound market.

When to Use Mezzanine & Preferred Equity in Seattle

Seattle's commercial real estate market, driven by technology, aerospace, healthcare, e-commerce, cloud computing, creates specific scenarios where mezzanine & preferred equity are the optimal financing choice:

  • High-leverage acquisitions
  • Development projects needing additional capital
  • Value-add strategies with equity gap
  • Recapitalizations and cash-out scenarios
  • Joint venture equity structures
  • Portfolio-level capital solutions

In the Seattle-Tacoma-Bellevue metro, mezzanine & preferred equity are particularly relevant given the market's 3.5% rent growth and 2.0% job growth, which support higher-leverage capital structures for competitive acquisitions.

Current Mezzanine Loan Rates in Seattle

As of 2026, mezzanine & preferred equity in the Seattle market are pricing at the following levels:

  • Rate Range: 10% - 18%
  • Loan Amount: $1M - $50M+
  • Term: 1 - 5 Years
  • Total Leverage: Up to 85-90% LTC
  • Recourse:

Rates in Seattle may vary from national averages based on local market conditions, property type, and sponsor experience. The Seattle market's 4.75%-5.25% multifamily cap rates and 4.75%-5.25% industrial cap rates influence lender pricing as they underwrite to specific debt yield and coverage targets.

Qualification Requirements

Qualifying for mezzanine & preferred equity in Seattle requires demonstrating both borrower strength and property fundamentals. Key requirements include:

  • Borrower Experience: Lenders evaluate your track record with similar assets in Seattle or comparable markets
  • Net Worth & Liquidity: Most lenders require net worth equal to the loan amount and 6-12 months of debt service in liquid reserves
  • Property Performance: Existing senior debt in place, property cash flow or value-add plan supporting the combined capital stack
  • Market Position: Asset location within Seattle's strongest submarkets, including South Lake Union tech campus, Bellevue East Side, Kent Valley industrial, Capitol Hill multifamily

Capital Sources for Mezzanine Loans in Seattle

The Seattle market offers access to a diverse set of capital sources for mezzanine & preferred equity:

  • Debt Funds
  • Private Equity Firms
  • Family Offices
  • Insurance Companies
  • Specialty Lenders

Each capital source has distinct appetites for property types, leverage levels, and borrower profiles. Working with a commercial mortgage broker who maintains relationships across all these capital sources ensures you're seeing the most competitive terms available in Seattle.

Exit Strategy Considerations

Mezzanine and preferred equity positions in Seattle are structured with clear exit timelines, typically aligning with the business plan execution period. The exit is usually through a refinance that consolidates the capital stack at a lower blended cost once the property's value has increased, or through a property sale that generates sufficient proceeds to repay all capital layers.

Given Seattle's 3.5% rent growth, well-executed value-add strategies can create the equity cushion needed to refinance into permanent financing that fully repays the mezzanine position.

Seattle Market Context

Seattle is one of the nation's premier technology hubs, home to Amazon, Microsoft, and a deep bench of tech companies driving demand for office, industrial, and multifamily space. The metro's constrained geography and strong job growth create persistent demand across all property types.

Understanding the local market dynamics is critical for structuring the right financing. The Seattle metro's key commercial neighborhoods include South Lake Union, Capitol Hill, Bellevue, Tacoma, Redmond, Shoreline, each with distinct property characteristics and tenant demand profiles.
